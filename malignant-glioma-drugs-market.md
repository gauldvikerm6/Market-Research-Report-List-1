<p><ul><li>Alkylating Agents</li><li>VEGF/VEGFR Inhibitors</li><li>Anti Angiogenic Drugs</li></ul></p>
<p><p>The malignant glioma drugs market comprises several key types, including alkylating agents, VEGF/VEGFR inhibitors, and anti-angiogenic drugs. Alkylating agents work by directly damaging DNA to inhibit tumor cell growth. VEGF/VEGFR inhibitors target the vascular endothelial growth factor pathway, reducing tumor blood supply. Anti-angiogenic drugs also aim to impede blood vessel formation, further restricting tumor development. Together, these therapies are crucial in managing malignant gliomas, aiming to improve patient outcomes and prolong survival.</p></p>
