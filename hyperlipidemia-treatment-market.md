<p><p>The hyperlipidemia treatment market is categorized into two main types: familial hyperlipidemia and acquired hyperlipidemia. Familial hyperlipidemia is a genetic disorder resulting in high cholesterol levels, necessitating specialized treatments such as statins and lipid-lowering therapies. In contrast, acquired hyperlipidemia arises from lifestyle factors, medical conditions, or medications, often treated with lifestyle modifications and drug therapies. Both categories demand tailored approaches, addressing the underlying causes and risk factors to effectively manage lipid levels and reduce cardiovascular disease risk.</p></p>
