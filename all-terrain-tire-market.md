<p><p>The all-terrain tire market features two primary types: bias tires and radial tires. Bias tires have layers of fabric plies that cross each other, offering durability and a sturdy build, making them ideal for rough surfaces. However, they may have less traction and heat dissipation than radial tires. Radial tires, with plies arranged at a right angle to the tread, provide better flexibility, improved fuel efficiency, and superior traction, making them popular for both on-road and off-road applications.</p></p>
