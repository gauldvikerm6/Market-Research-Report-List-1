<p><p>The Preclinical Positron Emission Tomography (PET) market is primarily segmented into structural and functional imaging types. Structural imaging focuses on visualizing anatomical details of biological specimens, providing high-resolution images of tissue morphology. In contrast, functional imaging assesses metabolic processes and physiological functions, allowing researchers to study disease mechanisms and drug effects in vivo. Both imaging types are crucial in advancing drug development and understanding complex biological processes in preclinical research, enhancing the ability to evaluate therapeutic interventions effectively.</p></p>
